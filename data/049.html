Jul 26, 1999		 Starshine is a "small, optically reflective spheroid" launched from Space Shuttle Discovery in June 1999. This mirror-covered satellite is naked-eye visible after dark to folks between the latitudes of 60 degrees North and 60 degrees South. The GSOC Satellite Predictions page allows you to enter your home latitude and longitude and get information about when and where Starshine might be visible to you. It also gives the current position of Starshine, as well as 10-day predictions for Mir, ISS, Columbia, and Iridium Flares, among lots of other things.
